The rulebook (what I actually follow)

Every format I post goes through the same filter. This list has been rewritten about a dozen times. If you steal it, at least pretend you came up with the last one yourself.

  1. The hook lives in the first three seconds.
    • No intro. No "hey guys."
    • Question first, face second, context third.
    • If the thumbnail doesn't work, the hook doesn't either.
  2. Strangers > scripts.
    • Real reactions out-rank rehearsed ones every time.
    • If a stranger says something better than I could have written, use their exact phrasing.
    • Never edit a stranger to look worse than they actually were.
  3. Generosity travels.
    • Shopping sprees land because the camera catches real surprise, not acting.
    • Always tip the employees who helped on location.
    • The stranger is the star, not me.
  4. Brand > views.
    • A viral clip that conflicts with the brand is a loss, not a win.
    • Faith-forward means I'd show my grandma the final cut.
    • Every collab has to pass the same test.
